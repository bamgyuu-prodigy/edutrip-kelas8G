Pelajaran Pendidikan Pancasila

Setelah Seminggu di Taiwan, Keelung. Aku telah belajar kalau Taiwan itu memiliki kebedaannya. Kebedaan yang telah membuatku terharu. Tidak seperti di Indonesia, Taiwan memiliki cara jalan yang teratur, seperti lalu lintas, tempat buang sampah, dan lain lainnya.

Lukang Old Street in Taiwan is a vibrant hub of cultural traditions that reflect its rich historical heritage. One of the most cherished customs revolves around temple worship, particularly at the Longshan Temple and the Mazu Temple, where locals honor Mazu, the sea goddess. Her birthday, celebrated on the 23rd day of the third lunar month, is marked by lively parades, lion dances, and religious processions that draw large crowds.

Food also plays a central role in Lukangâ€™s traditions, with the Old Street offering a variety of beloved local specialties such as ox tongue cakes (crispy pastries), chewy ba-wan (meat dumplings), shrimp monkeys, and handmade mochi. These dishes are often prepared using traditional methods passed down through generations.

The town also comes alive during festivals like the Dragon Boat Festival, featuring thrilling races and the making of rice dumplings, and the Lantern Festival, where handcrafted lanterns light up the streets and riddle games bring communities together. These traditions make Lukang Old Street a living museum of Taiwanese culture.
